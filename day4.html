Day 4 | 60 Days of Soul Mastery

Theme: Repentance

Moving from Pride to Reconciliation

Theme Scripture: 1 John 1:8-10
"If we confess our sins, He is faithful and just to forgive us our sins and to cleanse us from all unrighteousness." (v.9)

Good morning, The Bible says in Proverbs 28:13 that "He who conceals his sin does not prosper, but whoever confesses them finds mercy."

Friend, right from these two portions of scripture, we see light in taking the initiative of treasuring our relationship with God by accepting when we transgress His order. Confession clearly shows that we are hurt because of what has occurred, and we wish things to be different. It is submission to His supremacy.

You might have sinned, but this isn't too big for a relationship He killed for. Trust me. He is willing to have you back on the track—just drop the pride and say, "I know I messed up big time, but I am sorry and I know I am a work in progress." Then yearn to do things better.

Golden Nugget

"A repentant soul is expected of newer and stronger results after getting up and being cleansed."

Further Study

  • Psalm 32:5
  • Jeremiah 15:19-21
  • Psalm 9:10
  • Romans 3

Prayer

Thank you God for this truth. I know I have sinned, but I also know I am too precious to you. You said even if my sin is red like crimson, you are willing to wash it white like snow. Father, forgive me; I repent. Help me yield to the sonship I carry. Amen.
